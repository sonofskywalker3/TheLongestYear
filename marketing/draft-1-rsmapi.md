[Beta] The Longest Year — a roguelite time-loop over Community Center restoration (PC, SMAPI 4.0+); looking for difficulty/pricing/pacing feedback

BODY (markdown):
I just put up the first public beta (0.9.2) of a mod I've been building, **The Longest Year**, and I'd love feedback from people who actually dig into SMAPI mods.

**The hook:** restore the Community Center within a single year — or the Junimos rewind the seasons back to Spring 1 and you begin again, a little stronger. It turns year one into a roguelite loop:

- **Seasonal donation minimums.** Each season has a quota you must donate to the Center before it turns. Miss it and the year unwinds to Spring 1.
- **Junimo Points bank across loops.** Donations earn JP (scaled by item rarity and how late in the year you give). JP persists through resets.
- **Spend JP at the reset.** When the year unwinds, you cash in banked JP on carry-forward upgrades — skill levels, tool tiers, recipes, buildings, a kept pet — so each loop starts stronger than the last.
- **Weekly themes** give a paired bonus + liability you plan around — and you can lift that week's liability by donating all of its required bonus items.
- **Carryover surfaces** on the farm: a Bundle Log that tracks each season's goals, a Cookbook/Craftbook to bank recipes, and a Junimo Stash chest that survives resets.

It's **PC-only, needs SMAPI 4.0+, and wants a fresh save on the Standard farm** (it manages farm-type/intro for you). It's feature-complete for v1 and stable in testing — what it most needs now is balance feedback:

1. **Difficulty** — do the seasonal minimums feel fair? Which season wall hit hardest?
2. **Pricing** — are JP earnings and upgrade costs well-balanced?
3. **Pacing** — how many loops before it "clicked," and did carryover make later loops feel meaningfully stronger?

Bug reports with your `SMAPI-latest.txt` are gold. All the balance knobs live in `config.json` if you want to experiment.
